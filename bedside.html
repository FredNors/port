MedTech · EHR · Public Sector

Bedside

Reimagining the electronic health record for the tablet — giving clinicians better overview, smarter decision support, and less documentation.

The Challenge

A national tender for a next-generation patient record system — designed for tablet, built on modern technology, and aimed at making clinicians' daily work easier.

My Role

Design Lead. Concept development, clinical narrative, interactive prototype, and pitch delivery.

Outcome

Selected as one of 2 out of 35 companies to continue the dialogue with the national health ministry.

From tender to prototype in weeks

The timeline was brutal. We went from tender requirements to a fully clickable prototype in just a few weeks. Every screen, every interaction, every design decision was anchored in a clinical narrative that walked through a realistic patient journey — making sure we didn't just answer the tender requirements on paper, but showed how they'd work in practice.

The prototype wasn't a wireframe deck. It was a living, clickable experience that made the vision tangible and testable.

Five hours in front of a ministry

The concept was presented in a 5-hour pitch session to the national health ministry. That meant the design had to hold up under scrutiny, not just visually, but clinically and technically. Every interaction needed a rationale, every workflow needed to map to real clinical practice.

Being selected as one of just two companies out of 35 to continue wasn't just a win for the design. It validated the approach of leading with a strong clinical narrative backed by a prototype people could actually use.
