Digital Health · Pharma

MinLæge &
Gravitate Health

Replacing the paper leaflet with intelligent, personalised medication information — designed and validated inside Denmark's most-used health app.

The Challenge

Paper medication leaflets are one of the most important patient safety tools we have — and almost nobody reads them. Dense, multi-language documents in tiny fonts. The current format fails both patients and pharma.

My Role

Lead Designer. Concept development, prototyping, user testing, and validation across two research rounds within the MinLæge app.

Outcome

Validated with 135 patients through unmoderated testing and surveys, achieving strong SUS and NPS scores. Part of Gravitate Health — an EU-funded public-private partnership with 41 members across Europe and the US.

From paper to personal

The core idea is the G-Lens — a layer of intelligence on top of the standard electronic product information that filters and highlights what's relevant to you. Instead of scrolling through pages of dense text, the patient sees their leaflet adapted to their situation: their age, conditions, and other medications.

We designed and validated an implementation of the G-Lens inside MinLæge, Denmark's most widely used health app. The goal was to make the leaflet feel like something you'd actually want to read. Clear, scannable, and personal.

Testing with real patients

We ran the project in two rounds. The first focused on concept design, exploring how personalised leaflets could work within the existing MinLæge experience. The second was a larger validation round with 135 patients using unmoderated testing and surveys to measure usability, comprehension, and trust.

The results confirmed that patients found the personalised format significantly easier to navigate and more relevant than the traditional leaflet. They actually engaged with the information instead of ignoring it.
