How I work

From understanding
to outcomes

I start with the business, the market, and the people — then work towards a product that delivers measurable results.

01
Understand
Get to know the business, its market, and its users. What's the opportunity? What are the constraints? Build a clear foundation before anything else.
02
Vision & Strategy
Define where the product needs to go and why. Align stakeholders around a shared direction so every decision that follows has a rationale.
03
Discover & Design
Run a structured discovery process — research, ideation, prototyping, and testing with real users. Move fast, but validate before you commit.
04
Deliver & Measure
Ship, track, and learn. Design doesn't end at handoff — it ends when the outcomes show it's working.

About me

From diagnosis to product strategy — I figure out what's worth building

I'm Frederik — a medical doctor turned product designer based in Aarhus. I specialise in digital health, but I've also shipped products in fintech, energy, and food.

My background in medicine means I don't just design for users — I've been one. I've worked night shifts in the ER, consulted in general practice, and spent weeks doing fieldwork in hospital wards. That clinical experience shapes how I think about products: start with a real understanding of the problem, then design something that actually holds up in practice.

At work, I'm the AI go-to person. I created the classification and risk assessment framework that got ChatGPT approved at the highest company level — across all 1,200 employees — and I spend a lot of time helping colleagues make the most of new tools as they arrive. I also started a design and product book club, because I think the best designers never stop being curious about how other people think about the craft.

When I'm not working, I'm usually chasing waves, chasing kids, or planning the next ski trip. Married to a Norwegian, father of two.
