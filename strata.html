Strata Shopping
Experience

Revamping checkout and product discovery for a DTC brand

The Challenge

A growing DTC brand was losing 62% of users at checkout. Product discovery was buried under poor navigation and irrelevant search results, costing significant revenue.

My Role

Product Designer — UX audit, checkout redesign, search & browse redesign, A/B testing

Outcome

28% reduction in checkout drop-off, 18% increase in average order value, 2× conversion on search

Diagnosing
the drop-off

Before touching a single wireframe, we needed to understand precisely where and why users were leaving. A heuristic audit of the existing checkout flagged 23 distinct usability violations — from unclear error messaging to a four-step flow that asked for account creation before payment details.

Session recordings told the story more viscerally. We watched hundreds of users encounter the same three friction points repeatedly: a shipping estimate that only appeared after entering payment details, an address form with no autocomplete, and a cart that silently cleared after 30 minutes of inactivity. Each of these had a measurable exit rate spike.

We then ran a rapid round of usability testing with 8 participants across the existing flow to quantify the severity of each issue and prioritise where to focus the redesign effort. The three identified friction points accounted for an estimated 70% of the total drop-off.

Streamlining
the path to
purchase

The redesigned checkout collapsed four steps into a single scrollable page — contact, shipping, and payment visible simultaneously, with a persistent order summary anchored to the right. Users could see the full cost before committing to any field, eliminating the trust gap that had been driving abandonment.

Smart address autocomplete — powered by a postcode lookup API — reduced the address form from 6 fields to 3 for the majority of users. Error messages were redesigned to be inline, specific, and actionable rather than the generic modal that had previously interrupted the flow entirely.

Cart persistence was extended to 7 days with a clear "your cart is saved" indicator. Combined with a guest checkout option surfaced prominently at the start of the flow — rather than buried as an afterthought — these changes removed the final set of barriers between intent and purchase.
