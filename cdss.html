Health AI · Public Sector

CDSS (+prio)

Finding the right problem for an AI solution — turning a predictive model for acute hospitalisations into a tool clinicians actually want to use.

The Challenge

Enversion had built an AI model that could predict the risk of acute hospitalisation. The technology worked — but when pitched to GPs, there was little interest. The model needed a real problem to solve.

My Role

Product Designer. Market research, literature review, fieldwork, interviews, product vision and strategy.

Outcome

Identified multiple validated use cases — including triage of out-of-hours calls and resource prioritisation in emergency departments — directly impacting length of stay and admission rates.

Mapping the journey to hospitalisation

I started by mapping what actually happens before an acute admission. How does a deterioration begin at home? What are the touchpoints where the healthcare system could intervene earlier? And once a patient does arrive at the emergency department, what's the flow from arrival to admission?

Through fieldwork, interviews with clinicians, and a thorough literature review, I built a complete picture of the clinical pathway. That picture revealed several moments where a predictive risk score could genuinely change decisions and outcomes.

From model to use cases

The research uncovered multiple promising applications. One was prioritising incoming calls to the out-of-hours GP service, helping clinicians triage more effectively when demand is high. Another was prioritising resources for patients arriving at emergency departments, which has a direct impact on how long patients spend there and whether they end up being admitted.

The shift was fundamental: instead of trying to sell a technology to a user group that didn't need it, we found the contexts where the AI model solved a real, urgent problem.
