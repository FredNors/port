Food · Consumer

Arla Mat

Helping Arla Sweden turn a recipe library into a personal cooking experience — with measurable impact on engagement, retention, and app store ranking.

The Challenge

Arla Sweden has a huge library of recipes designed to inspire people to cook more and get better at it. But the experience wasn't personal enough — and engagement and retention were declining.

My Role

Design Lead. Product vision and strategy, concept development, A/B testing, and analytics.

Outcome

5× increase in cooking activity, retention stabilised after a declining trend, and the app went from a ranking of 72 to 33 in the App Store.

From recipes to personal cooking

The existing app was essentially a searchable recipe database. Functional, but passive. We defined a product vision and strategy that shifted the experience from browsing recipes to personal cooking — giving users insight into what they cook, helping them discover new things based on their preferences, and making the whole experience feel tailored rather than generic.

Every feature and experiment was tied to measurable outcomes for both the business and the users, making it easy to prioritise what to build and prove the value of what we shipped.

Measuring what matters

We ran structured A/B tests and tracked analytics closely to validate that changes were actually moving the numbers. The results spoke clearly: cooking activity increased 5×, a previously declining retention curve stabilised, and the App Store ranking improved from 72 to 33.

The shift wasn't about adding more content. It was about making the existing content work harder by connecting it to the individual user. Personalisation surfaced the right recipes at the right moment, and the my-page gave users a sense of progress and identity around their cooking.
