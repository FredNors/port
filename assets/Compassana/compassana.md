## The Challenge

Three of Switzerland's largest insurance companies and two of its biggest hospital chains joined forces to create a startup with an ambitious goal: a platform where patients experience a connected healthcare system, and where providers have visibility into what the others are doing. The vision was clear — but the product still needed to be defined.

## My Role

Product Designer — workshop facilitation, product vision and strategy.

## Outcome

Delivered a product vision and strategy that aligned five major healthcare organisations around a shared direction for the platform.

---

## Aligning five organisations

When your stakeholders include three insurers and two hospital chains, alignment is the product. Each organisation brought different priorities, different business models, and different ideas about what the platform should be. Before any design work could happen, everyone needed to agree on what problem they were solving and for whom.

Through structured workshops, we brought these perspectives together — mapping the patient journey across providers and identifying the moments where a shared platform could create the most value for patients and the business alike.

## From vision to strategy

The workshops produced a clear product vision and a strategy for how to get there. Not a feature list, but a shared understanding of what the platform should be, who it serves first, and what success looks like.

In a market as complex and regulated as Swiss private healthcare, getting five major players to commit to a single direction was as much the outcome as the strategy itself.
