## The Challenge

Millions of citizens fall into a treatment gap — too unwell for general practice, but not sick enough to be referred to a psychiatric department. Evidence-based therapy exists, but access is limited by geography, capacity, and wait times. Denmark needed a shared national platform to deliver digital therapy at scale.

## My Role

Product Designer — product strategy, user research, concept development, wireframing, prototyping, and user testing across three interconnected platforms.

## Outcome

Currently in development as the designated national solution for online mental health treatment in Denmark. Designed to meet WCAG accessibility and MDR medical device regulatory requirements.

---

## Three platforms, one experience

The platform is three products in one: an editor for building therapy programmes, a clinician tool for monitoring and communicating with patients, and a citizen app for completing treatment at your own pace.

Three very different user groups — content specialists, psychologists, and citizens in vulnerable situations. The challenge was creating a coherent design language across all three that feels like one product, despite fundamentally different needs and contexts.

## Designing within constraints

Every decision had to satisfy WCAG, MDR, and the realities of a public-private collaboration with many stakeholders. We moved from vision through structured discovery — mapping needs, aligning stakeholders, and narrowing scope to what delivered the most value first.

The result doesn't feel like a regulated product. It feels calm, clear, and human — which is exactly what someone in a difficult situation needs from a screen.
