## The Challenge

Patients are getting older and living with more conditions at once. That means parallel treatments, appointments across multiple departments, and different specialists who rarely see the full picture. For clinicians it's fragmented. For patients it's overwhelming. Nobody has a shared overview — and that leads to gaps, duplication, and worse outcomes.

## My Role

Lead Facilitator — research, stakeholder interviews, design sprint preparation, facilitation, and strategic roadmap.

## Outcome

Tested prototype of the first iteration and delivered a strategy for how to develop and anchor the concept politically and organisationally.

---

## Starting with a sprint

The project kicked off with a 5-day design sprint at Steno Diabetes Center Aarhus. Before the sprint, I ran an analysis of existing knowledge, interviewed clinicians and stakeholders across sectors, and mapped the most promising focus areas. That groundwork made sure we didn't waste the sprint exploring problems that had already been solved — or ones that couldn't be.

The sprint brought together people from different parts of the healthcare system who don't normally sit in the same room. Over five days we went from a broad problem space to a focused concept, a testable prototype, and a shared understanding of where this needed to go.

## From concept to strategy

The sprint delivered three things: prioritised focus areas, a first concept for the digital product, and a strategy for securing the political and financial backing needed to take it further.

What made this project meaningful wasn't just the prototype — it was creating alignment across organisational boundaries. Getting clinicians, administrators, and decision-makers to agree on what matters most for multimorbid patients, and giving them something tangible to rally behind.
