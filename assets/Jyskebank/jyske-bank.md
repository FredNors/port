## The Challenge

Becoming a customer at Jyske Bank was a painful experience. New customers were left waiting without knowing their status, bounced between employees, forced to chase the bank themselves, and spent too long getting the right documents submitted. Many lost patience before they even got started.

## My Role

Product Designer — user research, mystery shopping, journey mapping, design sprint facilitation, prototyping, and user testing.

## Outcome

The initial sprint led to a year-long engagement where the solution was developed and launched. It has since been sold to an organisation that provides solutions for 8 major Danish banks.

---

## Understanding the real experience

Before designing anything, we needed to understand what new customers were actually going through. Through user research and mystery shopping, we mapped the entire existing onboarding flow and identified where the biggest pain points were — the moments where frustration peaked and customers dropped off.

The insight wasn't that the process had too many steps. It was that customers felt invisible. They had no idea what was happening, when they'd hear back, or what they were supposed to do next.

## From sprint to scale

We ran a design sprint with the client to prioritise which pain points to tackle first, then developed and tested a prototype for a new onboarding experience. The sprint built enough conviction to turn a short engagement into a year-long collaboration.

The solution was developed, launched, and ultimately sold to an organisation serving 8 of Denmark's largest banks — turning a single client project into a product with much broader reach.
