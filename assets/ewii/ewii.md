## The Challenge

Ewii manages flexible energy assets — solar panels, wind turbines, and batteries — on behalf of their owners, optimising revenue across energy markets. Until now, customers received updates on their earnings via email and manual status reports. There was no self-service, no transparency, and no easy way to report operational issues. Ewii needed a platform that would give customers real insight into their assets and make the whole relationship more scalable.

## My Role

Product Designer — concept development, user flows, and wireframing.

## Outcome

Platform is currently being developed and launched in its first version.

---

## Making complexity understandable

Flexible energy is a complex domain. Multiple markets, fluctuating prices, different asset types, and a constant stream of performance data. The challenge wasn't a lack of information — it was too much of it. Customers needed to understand the value being created for them, see which technologies and markets were performing well, and know where things could be improved.

The design had to translate that complexity into something a non-expert could actually make sense of — clear enough to build confidence, detailed enough to be useful.

## Balancing insight and simplicity

The platform we designed strikes a balance between giving customers meaningful insight and not overwhelming them. It surfaces what matters — earnings, asset performance, market conditions — without requiring customers to understand the mechanics of energy trading.

It also makes it easy to report operational status on assets, helping customers avoid fines or missed revenue from unreported downtime. A practical feature that turns the platform from a nice-to-have dashboard into something genuinely useful.
