Research, Health Economics · 2017

Business Models
for Telemedicine

Why do most telemedicine projects die after the research phase? I studied the ones that didn't — and published what makes them commercially sustainable

A good clinical outcome is not enough. Without a viable business model, even the best telemedicine solution won't survive beyond the pilot phase.
— Finding from the research
The Challenge

Denmark produces a lot of telemedicine research. But very few projects make the leap from research to a sustainable product. Most die when the funding runs out. The question was: what separates the telemedicine solutions that actually survive?

My Role

Sole Researcher — project planning, identification and inclusion of cases, qualitative interviews, business model analysis, and peer-reviewed publication.

Outcome

Published in Health Services Management Research. Identified the business model patterns that distinguish commercially sustainable telemedicine from projects that remain stuck in the research phase.

Finding
the survivors

I started by scanning the Danish telemedicine landscape to identify solutions that had successfully made the transition from research project to commercially sustainable product — running on operational budgets, not grant funding. These were rare, which made them interesting.

For each case, I interviewed key people from the companies behind the products and mapped their business models using the Business Model Canvas framework. The goal was to find patterns — what did these companies do differently?

From interviews
to publication

I ran the entire project solo over six months: case identification, inclusion criteria, interviews, analysis, and writing. The findings were published in a peer-reviewed journal, contributing an evidence base for how telemedicine ventures can be designed for commercial viability from the start — not just clinical effectiveness.

The research challenged a common assumption in Danish healthcare innovation: that a good clinical outcome is enough. It's not. Without a viable business model, even the best telemedicine solution won't survive beyond the pilot phase.
