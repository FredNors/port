Mobile, iOS · 2024

Tempo Time
Tracking App

End-to-end design of a time tracker built for freelancers and indie teams

The Challenge

Freelancers were losing billable hours because existing tools were too heavy or too manual. The goal was a frictionless iOS app that captured time without breaking focus.

My Role

Solo designer — product strategy, UX, UI, iOS design system, prototype

Outcome

4.8 App Store rating at launch, 12,000 downloads in first month, featured by Apple in Productivity

Designing
for flow

The defining challenge of Tempo was interaction cost. Every tap, every mode switch, every confirmation dialog was a potential moment for a user to abandon the app and simply not log the time. We set a goal of zero — any task achievable in the tool should be achievable in a single tap from the home screen.

One-tap timers were the cornerstone of this philosophy. A user opens the app and sees their recent projects; they tap one and time starts. No project selection screen, no naming dialog, no confirmation. The timer runs in the background and surfaces as a persistent live activity on the Dynamic Island.

Smart suggestions took this further. By reading the user's calendar with explicit permission, Tempo could infer what they were likely working on and surface it at the top of the list. In user testing, this reduced time-to-start from an average of 8 seconds to under 2.

The details
that matter

The micro-details of an iOS app are often where the experience lives or dies. For Tempo, haptics were designed as a first-class citizen — the start and stop of a timer have distinct, satisfying feedback that reinforces the binary nature of time tracking: you're either on or you're off.

Typography hierarchy was refined through four rounds of testing. The running timer display needed to be glanceable from a distance, so we settled on a tabular Fraunces numeral at 72pt as the focal point, with secondary information stepping down in a clear visual hierarchy.

Dark mode wasn't an afterthought — it was designed in parallel with the light theme from day one, with a semantic token system ensuring every component adapted correctly. The widget collection — four sizes, two themes — was designed to feel at home on any home screen aesthetic.
